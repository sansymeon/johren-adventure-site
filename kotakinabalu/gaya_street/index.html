Kota Kinabalu Gaya Street and Beyond

A small introduction to places you may pass every day.

Starting point

Gaya Street Sunday Market

Every Sunday, Gaya Street transforms into a lively market lined with small stalls selling crafts, local snacks, and handmade goods. It’s a familiar weekly rhythm where locals and visitors drift through the old street together.

Everyday place

Old Jesselton — where Gaya Street began

Once the heart of the old town, this area now sits slightly outside the main tourist flow. Buses no longer stop here, but the value remains — local food, everyday flavours, and a growing mix of modern cafés offering a quiet pause from the heat.

By the sea

Jesselton Port

Your excursions to the islands start here — Manukan, Sapi & Gaya. Just offshore from Kota Kinabalu, these nearby islands offer an easy escape from the city. Manukan and Sapi are relaxed and accessible, known for beaches, swimming, and casual day trips. Gaya Island sits a little apart, home to forest trails and high-end resorts — the quieter, more polished side of the bay.

Station spot

The Floating Mosque

Set beside the sea, the mosque appears to rest on the water when the tide is high. Open and welcoming in design, it’s a quiet landmark where daily life, faith, and the coastline meet — especially peaceful in the early morning or near sunset.
